Physical Therapy

Excellence in physical therapy is our way of life at Elder Care. We provide the highest quality physical therapy treatments to promote wellness for adults in our community.

Why choose Elder Care Physical Therapy?

We recognize the unique physical needs of adults and tailor individual programs to best meet their needs. Our staff is experienced in treating adults of all ages in a one-on-one, patient-centered environment that is personal and interactive. While you may have the same diagnosis as someone else, you have your own set of circumstances surrounding that issue. Your treatment may look very different.

Our fun-filled atmosphere encourages a sense of community and fellowship during your rehabilitation. Our state-of-the-art clinic offers Olympic-caliber fitness equipment with pneumatic resistance, Nu-Step machines, Sci-Fit recumbent bicycles, a Total Gym, treadmills, therabands, mat tables, physioballs, free weights, pulleys, stairs and more. Our ultimate goal is to help you with your current issue,while providing a structure for ongoing physical well-being.

Choose Excellence, Choose Elder Care.

What are the benefits of physical therapy?

Physical therapy is beneficial for a variety of reasons including physical fitness, improved mobility, injury prevention, surgery recovery and more. Our experts can assist people who are struggling with any of the following problems:

  • Balance difficulty
  • Functional decline
  • Muscle and joint inflammation
  • Muscle weakness
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Osteoporosis
  • Overuse injuries
  • Cardiopulmonary pathology
  • Parkinson’s Disease
  • Poor endurance or fitness level
  • Poor soft tissue flexibility/elasticity
  • Post-joint replacement
  • Pre- and post-operative orthopedic injuries
  • Restricted joint motion
  • Spinal pathologies
  • Stroke/CVA
  • Surgery recovery
  • Vestibular disorders and dizziness

Do you feel like you are losing your balance?

Falls in seniors over the age of 65 is a serious health risk and can threaten their independence. Elder Care’s Physical Therapy department offers free balance assessments called “Stay on Your Feet.” These assessments only take 10-15 minutes to perform in the clinic. Those who participate will receive information on fall prevention, physical activity and home modifications. If physical therapy is indicated, we will work to set up a more comprehensive evaluation as well.

Please contact Elder Care’s Physical Therapy Department at 918-766-0391 or email Josh Lindblom at jlindblom@abouteldercare.org to schedule your free “Stay on Your Feet” appointment.

What is Direct Access to Physical Therapy?

Many insurance providers, including Medicare, have allowed for a physical therapy evaluation to be performed without referral for years. However, under our state practice act, we could not provide any skilled treatment following that evaluation without permission from your physician. Now, Direct Access to Physical Therapy empowers you with the freedom to participate in a comprehensive evaluation PLUS appropriate treatment from a licensed physical therapist for up to 30 days without a referral from your physician. If care will extend beyond 30 days, physician recommendation will be required at that point. While the majority of our clients continue to arrive via the traditional referral route, Direct Access allows you to receive treatment in a timelier manner. YOU decide where you would like physical therapy.

Choose Excellence, Choose Elder Care.

What can I expect from my sessions?

On the first day, a licensed physical therapist will perform a comprehensive evaluation to understand your problem and how it is affecting your daily life. Family members are welcome to accompany clients and add details or support if you wish. During this evaluation, your therapist will establish an individualized plan of care, as well as the expected goals.

Each subsequent session will include one-on-one care with your therapist for up to an hour. Treatment duration is different for each individual. However, the average treatment cycle is two to three visits per week for one month.

Re-evaluations are performed every 10 visits or 30 days (whichever comes first) until the client has met all of their functional goals or is ready to transition to our Aftercare Program.

Are there any additional benefits?

Yes. Elder Care physical therapy offers our Aftercare Program exclusively to our physical therapy clients. Following skilled physical therapy discharge, the Aftercare Program allows clients to continue to utilize the gym and all of the fitness equipment to extend the benefits of their treatment. Our therapists supply each client with an individual exercise routine for ongoing maintenance of the strength and balance gained in therapy. Long-term, continued maintenance therapy is the key to remaining physically active and independent at home. There are two ways to participate in our Aftercare Program.

Independent Aftercare

Independent Aftercare does not include direct supervision or assistance from a staff member and may be completed at your convenience during our regular business hours from 8 a.m. to 12 p.m. and 1 p.m. to 5 p.m., Monday through Friday. However, our therapy staff is always available to answer questions that you may have regarding the exercise program or equipment. This program requires pre-payment of $25 per month.

Assisted Aftercare

Assisted Aftercare includes scheduled individual sessions with a staff member, not a licensed therapist. The staff will assist or direct you through your exercise program that was established by your physical therapist. Your program may be adapted at the discretion of the trained staff member after consultation with the therapist. This program requires a pre-payment of $240 for 12 scheduled sessions, usually 2 or 3 times per week.
